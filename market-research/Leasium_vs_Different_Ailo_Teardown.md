# Leasium vs :Different & Ailo — Teardown

Last updated: 2026-05-31
Companion to: `Leasium_vs_PropertyMe_Strategy.md`, `Leasium_vs_ReLeased_Cost_Comparison.md`,

## Bottom line (the sharp finding)

The Re-Leased doc flagged `:Different` and `Ailo` as the next AU comparisons. After the teardown,
the headline is **clarifying, and good news for Leasium**:

> **Neither :Different nor Ailo is a self-managing-owner SaaS rival.** Ailo is a modern *agency
> platform* (a PropertyMe challenger). :Different is a *managed service* (done-for-you), now largely
> white-labelling its service delivery to agencies. So the "great software sold directly to a DIY
> owner" lane — Leasium's target — is **essentially uncontested in AU by these three.**

Leasium's real competitor in that lane is therefore **not another tool** — it's the **decision to
outsource** (hand it to an agency or :Different) and **inertia** (spreadsheets + the accountant).
That sharpens the positioning: Leasium competes against the **management fee**, not against a SaaS price.

## :Different — teardown

**What it is:** a tech-enabled property-management **service** ("Property Management with 100%
transparency") — a purpose-built owner app + an in-house office/field team that does end-to-end
leasing, inspections, maintenance, payments and compliance *for* the owner. Legal entity: Link Realty
International Pty Ltd.

**Who it's for / model:** investment owners who **don't want to self-manage** but want better
transparency than a traditional agent ("without paying more"). It charges a **management fee** (a
service, not a SaaS seat). Critically, it has pivoted hard to a **B2B / white-label** arm —
":Different for Agencies": PM **service delivery**, grow-your-rent-roll, transparent rent-roll
reporting, **agency-branded experience**, and a **guaranteed-margin commercial model**. So today it's
as much an *outsourced back-office for agencies* as a direct-to-owner brand.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Strengths** | Real owner-app transparency; "done-for-you" removes all effort; the white-label arm gives it an agency distribution channel. |
| **Weaknesses (Leasium's openings)** | It's a **service with a management fee** — structurally far more expensive than SaaS; the owner **gives up control** and the fee; it's a people-business (margin/scale limits); residential-leaning. |
| **Threat to Leasium** | A **substitute**, not a rival tool: the owner who'd use Leasium might instead just outsource to :Different. |
| **Counter-positioning** | *"Keep control and keep the fee. Leasium makes self-managing as easy as outsourcing — for a small monthly software fee, not a slice of every month's rent."* |

## Ailo — teardown

**What it is:** a modern **all-in-one B2B platform for property-management businesses** ("break free
from your legacy tech stack") — money, communication, compliance, inspections, workflows and insights
in one product — plus a **consumer app** for the owners/renters/tradespeople of those agencies.
Explicitly frames PropertyMe-style tools as "legacy."

**Who it's for / model:** **agencies** (esp. growing ones, ~100–500 doors) that want a better UX than
the incumbents; **custom per-property pricing**; native apps; **~200,000+** PMs/owners/renters on the
platform. Signature capability: **real-time money on the NPP** — rent in, disbursements and trade
payments out, instantly.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Strengths** | Best-in-class **real-time NPP payments** + owner/renter transparency; modern UX; consumer app is a genuine experience edge; the "outcomes not features" positioning. |
| **Weaknesses (Leasium's openings)** | It's an **agency** product — an owner only touches Ailo if their *agency* runs it; per-property pricing scales with the rent roll; residential-leaning; not sold to a DIY owner. |
| **Threat to Leasium** | **Low directly** — different buyer (agency). Indirectly it **raises the owner-experience bar** (real-time payments, app transparency) that Leasium must match, and it's the incumbent Leasium would meet **only if** it later attacks the small-agency segment. |
| **What to borrow** | The **NPP real-time payments + instant owner transparency** model — this is the AU expectation now; Leasium's tenant-payments P1 should hit this bar (review-first on reconciliation). |

## The competitive map (who actually serves whom)

| Lane | Who's there | Leasium's stance |
|---|---|---|
| **Software sold to the DIY owner (2–20)** | *Open* — DoorLoop/Buildium are US/not AU-localised; spreadsheets | **Leasium's lane.** Win it. |
| **Agency PM software (trust accounting)** | PropertyMe (incumbent), **Ailo** (modern challenger) | Don't enter first; borrow Ailo's payments/UX bar. |
| **Done-for-you managed service** | **:Different**, traditional agents | The substitute to beat — compete on *control + fee*. |
| **Commercial/enterprise PM** | Re-Leased | Price ceiling / positioning foil (small commercial is your niche). |

## Implications — sharpen the PropertyMe strategy

1. **The lane is genuinely open.** No one sells great software *directly to the self-managing 2–20
   owner* in AU. That validates the beachhead; the risk is demand-side (will owners self-manage?),
   not a competing product — so the **self-management rate** remains the key lever to test.
2. **Compete against the management fee, not a SaaS price.** Frame Leasium vs :Different/agency:
   *keep control, keep the ~7–8% management fee, pay a small monthly software fee instead.* The cost
   gap is the story (same mechanism as the Re-Leased comparison, but vs a *service* fee here).
3. **Match the Ailo money bar.** Real-time **NPP** rent-in / fast reconcile is now the AU expectation;
   build Leasium's tenant-payments P1 to that standard (review-first), or owners will feel it's behind.
4. **Own what none of them do:** document-first self-serve onboarding, Xero-open ledger, commercial
   depth, and "software not a service." That combination is uncontested.
5. **Still to teardown:** **Kolmeo** (AU agency platform) and **DoorLoop/Buildium** (US self-serve) —
   the latter are the closest *philosophical* analogues to Leasium's lane, just not AU-localised.
