# Leasium vs Re-Leased — Cost Comparison

**Bottom line:** Re-Leased and Leasium aren't really the same product. Re-Leased is **commercial / mixed-use** software for mid-market and enterprise managers (it openly says you'll "get the most value… if you have a commercial or mostly commercial portfolio"), sold on **quote-based premium pricing** with **mandatory paid implementation**, aimed at portfolios of **50 to 20,000+ leases**. Leasium targets the opposite end — residential SMB self-managers running **2–20 properties** on a transparent low per-tenant price with no setup. So treat Re-Leased as a **price ceiling and positioning foil**, not a head-to-head rival.

> ⚠️ Re-Leased does **not publish pricing** — every plan is "Request a quote." The dollar figures below are third-party estimates (ITQlick, DoorLoop, G2/Capterra) and are editable in the model. Replace them with a real quote when you have one.

## What you're actually comparing

| | Re-Leased | Leasium |
|---|---|---|
| Segment | Commercial & mixed-use | Residential SMB |
| Typical portfolio | 50–20,000+ leases | 2–20 properties |
| Buyer | PM firms, commercial landlords, institutions | Self-managing landlords & small agencies |
| Pricing | Quote-based, premium | Transparent per-tenant |
| Setup fee | Mandatory, paid (~40% of yr-1 sub, per ITQlick) | $0, self-serve |
| Min practical size | ~50 leases | 1+ property |

## Cost at small portfolios (the segment Leasium actually serves)

Using illustrative inputs — Leasium **$3/tenant/mo**, Re-Leased est. **$3.50/unit/mo** with a **$150/mo floor** and **$2,500 implementation**:

| Portfolio | Leasium / yr | Re-Leased yr 1 | Re-Leased effective $/unit/mo |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5 units | $180 | ~$4,300 | ~$72 |
| 10 units | $360 | ~$4,300 | ~$36 |
| 20 units | $720 | ~$4,300 | ~$18 |
| 50 units | $1,800 | ~$4,600 | ~$8 |
| 100 units | $3,600 | ~$6,700 | ~$6 |

The mechanism is the story: below ~43 units, Re-Leased's **monthly minimum plus one-off implementation** dominate, so a 5–20 unit landlord would pay an effective **$18–$72 per unit per month** — if Re-Leased even accepted them. Leasium stays flat at its per-tenant rate with nothing upfront. The gap closes around ~100 units, which is well past Leasium's 2–20 target and into Re-Leased's home turf.

## Takeaways

Re-Leased validates that managers will pay real money for good property software, but its floor-plus-setup model and commercial focus leave the residential long tail — your 2–20 segment — underserved and overpriced. That's the wedge: transparent pricing, zero setup, self-serve onboarding, residential-first. For a genuine like-for-like residential benchmark, the next comparisons to run are **PropertyMe, :Different and Ailo** (AU) and **DoorLoop / Buildium** (US) — those compete for the same buyer Leasium does.
