# Leasium vs PropertyMe — How to Take Them On

Last updated: 2026-05-31
Companion to: `Leasium_Market_Sizing_Brief.md`, `Leasium_SEQ_Brisbane_Market.md`,
`Leasium_vs_ReLeased_Cost_Comparison.md`, `docs/doorloop-benchmark-2026-05-31.md` §14.

## Bottom line (the thesis)

**Do not fight PropertyMe head-on.** PropertyMe owns AU residential property *management
agencies* — its moat is **trust accounting** (holding third-party money under state law),
~**two-thirds of AU investment properties**, 6,000+ agencies, and years of switching cost.
Attacking that is a money-fire.

PropertyMe is the **right foil, wrong fight**. Its entire product assumes an *agency
managing other people's money*. That makes it structurally clumsy — or irrelevant — for the
segment your own market model already targets: **owners who manage their own property
(2–20), who don't do trust accounting and don't want an agency-shaped tool.** That long tail
is where you win, and it's the same wedge that beats Re-Leased from below.

> One line: **PropertyMe is built for agencies; Leasium is built for owners who manage their
> own — residential *and* commercial — with the paperwork done for them.**

## Why head-on loses (know the moat you're avoiding)

- **Trust accounting is a regulatory moat, not just a feature.** Agencies are legally locked
  into a compliant trust ledger; ripping it out mid-year is unthinkable. You'd be cloning
  PropertyMe's hardest, least-fun surface to win customers who have every reason not to move.
- **Distribution + habit.** 2/3 of the market, deep REA/Domain/forms integrations, an
  installed base trained on it. New entrants don't out-execute that frontally.
- **It's a different buyer.** PropertyMe's customer is a *principal/PM at an agency*. SKJ —
  and your GTM target — is the *owner*. Selling owners an agency tool is friction on both sides.

## The opening — where PropertyMe is structurally weak

| PropertyMe assumption | The gap it creates |
|---|---|
| You are an **agency** holding third-party money | **Self-managing owners** don't need trust accounting at all — the core is dead weight to them |
| **Residential** letting is the world | **Commercial / mixed** owners get thin support (outgoings/CAM, options, make-good) at the small end |
| Its **own closed ledger** is the accounting system | Owners who already run **Xero + an accountant** must duplicate or abandon it |
| Data is **keyed in** by trained staff | A self-manager faces **agency-grade data entry** with no agency staff to do it |
| Priced/onboarded for **agencies** (per-property + onboarding, plan tiers) | The 2–20 owner is **over-served and over-charged** for what they need |

Re-Leased leaves the same long tail underserved from the *commercial-enterprise* top;
PropertyMe leaves it underserved from the *residential-agency* side. **The 2–20 owner-managed
segment is squeezed between two incumbents that both built for someone else.** That's the lane.

## WHERE TO START FOCUS

**Beachhead: self-managing 2–20 owners in Greater Brisbane.** (Straight from your model:
~48,000 self-managing tenancies / ~21,000 customers in Brisbane alone; win half ≈ ~24,000
tenancies in one metro — enough to prove the model before spending a dollar on the rest of SEQ.)

**The hero wedge — lead with the one thing PropertyMe can't easily copy: document-first
onboarding.** The demo that wins this segment:

> *"Drop your lease pack (and a bank statement / rent schedule). Leasium reads them and
> builds your properties, units, tenants, leases, rent roll and key dates in minutes — you
> review and approve. No agency, no data entry, no setup fee."*

That is Smart Intake pointed straight at the incumbent's worst moment (onboarding/data
entry). It's a 10x experience, and it's the hardest thing for a closed-ledger agency tool to
retrofit. **Make self-serve, document-first onboarding the product's front door — it is the
single highest-leverage focus.**

**Three must-haves to be credible at the beachhead** (all already on the roadmap):
1. **Owner + tenant experience** — the People hub (Owner entity shipped) + owner/tenant
   portals so the owner self-manages and the tenant self-serves.
2. **AU money rails** — PayTo / PayID / BPAY / direct debit so tenants actually pay through
   Leasium (PropertyMe's MePay/Trustless is the bar; you reconcile review-first via Basiq/Xero).
3. **Mobile (PWA first)** — owners and tenants on a phone.

Everything else (inspections, deep automation) comes after the beachhead converts.

## WHAT TO TARGET

**Segment (in order):**
1. **Self-managing residential owners, 2–20, Brisbane** — the volume play (your headline number).
2. **Small *commercial / mixed* self-managing owners** — your unfair advantage. PropertyMe is
   residential-weak and Re-Leased is enterprise-priced; nobody serves the small commercial owner
   well. This is literally what Leasium was built for (SKJ). Land it as the differentiated niche.
3. **Small agencies (2–20 doors) — later, and only if the owner motion works.** This is
   PropertyMe's turf; don't open this front first.

**Buyer pains to hammer:** onboarding/data-entry friction; paying agency prices for an
agency tool you don't need; a closed ledger fighting your Xero/accountant; zero commercial
depth at the small end; setup fees and plan-tier upsells.

**Build (target):** document-first self-serve onboarding · owner/tenant portals · AU payment
rails · review-first arrears/comms/lease-event automation · mobile/PWA · light inspections ·
Xero-native accounting · commercial-lease depth (your moat).

**Do NOT build (PropertyMe's turf / wrong customer):** statutory **trust accounting**, the
**agency rent-roll CRM** ("Grow"), and deep residential-letting/agency workflows. Building these
means fighting the moat on the moat's terms.

**Positioning lines:**
- "Built for owners, not agencies."
- "Your paperwork becomes your portfolio — drop the documents, we do the data entry."
- "Works with your Xero and your accountant — not a closed ledger."
- "Residential *and* commercial. Transparent per-tenant pricing. No setup fee. Cancel anytime."

## HOW TO SHIFT (internal tool → product that takes share)

- **Phase 0 — now (internal-first, ~6 months).** Harden on SKJ's real commercial portfolio:
  data integrity, audit, Smart Intake accuracy, the review-first guardrails. This is also your
  proof that the *commercial* self-managing-owner case is real. Keep deprioritising branding/
  external polish per the internal-first call.
- **Phase 1 — beachhead (Brisbane, owner-managed).** Ship the front door (document-first
  self-serve onboarding) + the three must-haves; recruit a handful of Brisbane self-managing
  owners (start with commercial/mixed where you're strongest, then residential). Instrument
  activation: *documents dropped → portfolio built → first rent reconciled*. That funnel is the
  whole game.
- **Phase 2 — density + loop (SEQ).** Turn on the **maintenance-partner network** — Brisbane's
  rental stock alone is a **~$740M/yr maintenance wallet across ~950k jobs**; even 50% of the
  self-managing segment routes **~72,500 jobs/yr** to preferred trades. Job density in one metro
  is a moat PropertyMe doesn't lead with and a growth loop (owners get faster maintenance; trades
  get volume; both pull in more owners).
- **Phase 3 — widen.** More AU metros, deeper automation/AI, and only then evaluate the small-
  agency segment (where you'd finally meet PropertyMe — by which point you have a doc-AI + Xero +
  commercial-depth product they can't match in a quarter).

The product gap between "SKJ commercial tool today" and "owner-managed product" is mostly the
**People hub + portals + AU payments + mobile + self-serve onboarding** — i.e. the existing
DoorLoop-benchmark roadmap. So *the shift is the roadmap you're already on*, re-pointed at
self-managing owners instead of only SKJ.

## Moats you're building (that PropertyMe can't cheaply copy)

- **Document-AI (Smart Intake)** on an **open Xero** ledger — the opposite of a closed trust
  ledger; hard to retrofit.
- **Commercial + residential in one** at the small end — neither incumbent does this.
- **Transparent, $0-setup, self-serve** pricing — structurally hard for an agency-priced incumbent.
- **Brisbane maintenance-partner density** — a local network effect.
- **Review-first trust** — owners stay in control; AI drafts, owner approves.

## Pricing (from your model — keep it the weapon)

Transparent **per-tenant** (illustrative **$3/tenant/month**), **$0 setup**, self-serve, cancel
anytime. At 50% of the AU self-managing 2–20 segment (~223k tenancies) that's ~**$8M ARR** at
$3; Brisbane-first proves unit economics on ~24k tenancies first. The pricing *is* the wedge
against both PropertyMe (agency plans + onboarding) and Re-Leased (floor + paid implementation).

## Honest risks / what to watch

- **The nearer rivals aren't PropertyMe — they're `:Different` and `Ailo`** (and Kolmeo), who
  already chase the self-managing/owner-experience segment in AU. Your real head-to-head is with
  them; PropertyMe is the incumbent you *position against*, not the one you'll bump into first.
  Run those two comparisons next (the Re-Leased doc already flagged them).
- **PropertyMe could ship a "self-manager lite" tier.** Likely eventually, but it fights their
  agency DNA and trust-accounting core; you have a head start on document-AI + commercial + Xero.
- **Self-management rate is the most sensitive lever** (25–33%) — the model's own caveat; the
  beachhead math holds across that range but watch it.
- **Trust:** owners handing you their leases/finances need security + the review-first promise
  front and centre. Make it a feature, not fine print.

## First 90 days (concrete)

1. **Build the front door:** self-serve, document-first onboarding ("drop docs → portfolio")
   as the public entry — the anti-PropertyMe demo. Instrument the activation funnel.
2. **Stand up owner + tenant portals** on the new Owner entity; pick the AU payment rail
   (Monoova / Zai / Stripe AU) and wire a review-first reconcile.
3. **Recruit 3–5 Brisbane self-managing owners** (commercial/mixed first), white-glove them in,
   watch where Smart Intake breaks on real third-party documents.
4. **Sign one maintenance-partner** (plumber/electrician network) on the Brisbane-density pitch.
5. **Run the `:Different` and `Ailo` teardowns** to sharpen positioning against the *real* rivals.
