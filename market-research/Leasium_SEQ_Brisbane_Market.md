# Leasium — SE Queensland (Brisbane-first) Market & Maintenance Partners

**Approach:** prove it in Brisbane, then roll out across SEQ. Everything below is anchored on the RTA's hard count — **631,805 active rental bonds in Queensland** at 30 June 2025 — then apportioned to Greater Brisbane and SEQ and run through the same segment model as the national work. Detail and live inputs are on the new **"SE Queensland"** tab of `Leasium_Market_Sizing.xlsx`.

## The rental market

| | Greater Brisbane (launch) | SE Queensland (rollout) | Queensland |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total rental tenancies | ~316,000 | ~461,000 | 631,805 |
| Self-managed (all) | ~95,000 | ~138,000 | ~190,000 |
| Agent-managed | ~221,000 | ~323,000 | ~442,000 |

Brisbane holds roughly half of Queensland's rental tenancies; SEQ holds about three-quarters. (Those shares are apportionments anchored to population/dwelling weight — swap in RTA postcode/LGA data to firm them up.)

## Leasium's two target segments

| | Greater Brisbane | SE Queensland |
|---|---|---|
| Self-managing 2–20 landlords (customers) | ~21,000 | ~31,000 |
| Their tenancies (TAM) | ~48,000 | ~71,000 |
| **Tenancies at 50% capture** | **~24,000** | **~35,000** |
| Small PM / real estate agencies | ~1,120 | ~1,540 |

So Brisbane alone is a ~48,000-tenancy self-managing segment — enough to validate the model before spending a dollar on the rest of SEQ. Win half of it and you're managing ~24,000 tenancies in one metro.

## The maintenance-partner opportunity (why Brisbane-first works)

This is where a dense single-metro launch pays off — maintenance partners care about job density, and Brisbane delivers it.

| | Greater Brisbane | SE Queensland |
|---|---|---|
| Maintenance jobs / year (whole market) | ~950,000 | ~1.38M |
| Rental maintenance spend / year (whole market) | **~$740M** | **~$1.08B** |
| Construction businesses (ABS basis) | ~43,500 | ~63,500 |
| Maintenance-relevant trade businesses (est.) | ~17,000 | ~25,000 |
| **Maintenance jobs Leasium routes at 50% capture** | **~72,500 / yr** | **~106,000 / yr** |

The pitch to a trade partner is concrete: Brisbane's rental stock alone throws off a **~$740M/year maintenance wallet** across **~950,000 jobs**, and even a 50% share of just the self-managing segment lets Leasium route **~72,500 jobs a year** to preferred partners. You don't need a national footprint to make a plumber or electrician network say yes — you need density in one city, which Brisbane gives you.

## How to read this

The tenancy and segment numbers are solid (RTA-anchored). The maintenance layer is a **planning estimate** — jobs-per-tenancy (3/yr), maintenance as % of rent (7.5%), and the maintenance-relevant trade share (40% of construction firms) are assumptions, all editable on the tab. Treat the maintenance wallet and job-volume figures as order-of-magnitude sizing for partner conversations, not forecasts. Agency counts are directory scrapes (Brisbane ~1,120, QLD ~2,110).
