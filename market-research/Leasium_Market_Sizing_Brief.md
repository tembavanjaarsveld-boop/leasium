# Leasium — SMB Property Manager Market Sizing

**Target:** owners and managers of **2–20 properties**, sized as two non-overlapping segments per country — (A) **self-managing landlords** who own 2–20 properties, and (B) **small property-management agencies**. The unit throughout is a **tenancy** = one dwelling under management = your per-tenant billing unit. Companion model: `Leasium_Market_Sizing.xlsx` (edit the blue cells to flex market share, price, and the key assumptions).

## Australia (the ask — high confidence)

Australia has **~2.27 million** people declaring rental income (ATO 2021–22, the latest data, released June 2024). Of those, **~647,500 own 2 or more properties** — this is the raw multi-property landlord base. Almost none own more than ~20, so this whole group sits inside your 2–20 target.

Roughly **30%** of landlords self-manage (sources cluster at 25–33%). Applying that gives the two segments:

| Australia segment | Target customers | Tenancies in segment (TAM) | At 50% share |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A. Self-managing landlords (2–20)** | ~194,000 landlords | ~446,000 tenancies | **~223,000 tenancies** (~97,000 customers) |
| **B. Small PM agencies** | ~11,800 businesses | ~2.03M tenancies | ~1.0M tenancies (~5,900 businesses) |

**So: capturing 50% of the self-managing 2–20 landlord market in Australia ≈ ~223,000 tenancies.** That is the headline number for your cost-per-tenant maths. The agency segment is far larger on a tenancy basis (~2M agent-managed doors, ~172 per agency) but those customers already pay an agent — a different sale than the DIY landlord.

Sanity check: ~446k self-managed multi-property + ~428k self-managed single-property + ~2.03M agent-managed ≈ 2.9M total private rental dwellings. ✓

## New Zealand (medium confidence)

~600,000 rental dwellings (StatsNZ; 410,904 active bonds as a conservative floor). ~80% of NZ landlords own a single property, and self-management is much higher than Australia — professional managers handle only ~42% of rentals, so **~58% self-manage**.

| NZ segment | Target customers | Tenancies in segment (TAM) | At 50% share |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A. Self-managing landlords (2–20)** | ~43,500 landlords | ~174,000 tenancies | **~87,000 tenancies** |
| **B. Small PM agencies** | ~2,000–7,900 (≈4,000) | ~300,000 tenancies | ~150,000 tenancies |

## United States (order-of-magnitude only)

~49.5M rental units; ~10.5M individual "mom-and-pop" landlords; ~15.9M units sit in individually-owned 1–4 unit buildings, and small US landlords overwhelmingly self-manage (~75%).

| US segment | Target customers | Tenancies in segment (TAM) | At 50% share |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A. Self-managing landlords (2–20)** | ~5M landlords | ~11.9M tenancies | **~6.0M tenancies** |
| **B. Small PM agencies** | ~238,000 businesses | ~22M units | ~11M units |

The US is huge but extremely fragmented — treat 50% as purely illustrative; realistic early share is a fraction of a percent.

## What this means for cost-per-tenant

The model multiplies *tenancies at your chosen share × price per tenant × 12* to show annual revenue. As a reference point, 50% of the Australian self-managing 2–20 segment (~223,000 tenancies) at an illustrative $3/tenant/month is ~$8.0M ARR; the agency segment would add materially more. Set your own price in the Excel to back out the unit economics — and note that **the self-management rate is the single most sensitive lever** for the landlord segment, so test the 25–33% range.

## Confidence & caveats

Australia is built bottom-up from ATO tax data (every landlord who declares income) — high confidence. NZ landlord/PM splits are estimated from MBIE ranges — medium. The US 2–20 count is a rough estimate — low. The ATO reports "property interests," not dwellings, so a 0.88 factor removes double-counting of jointly-owned properties. "Small PM agency" taken literally as 2–20 doors is a tiny new-entrant niche; the agency rows therefore size SMB agencies broadly (priced per managed door).
