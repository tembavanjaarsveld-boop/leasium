# Leasium → Rename Brief

**Date:** 2026-06-21
**For:** Temba (operator/founder)
**Scope of this doc:** collision evidence, a vetted shortlist of replacement names, and a staged plan to change the name across the back end, front end, data, and external services. No code or config has been changed.

---

## 1. The problem in one line

"Leasium" is not distinctive. There are at least four other live "Leasium" entities, and one of them is a near-identical AI-leasing product on an almost-identical domain. For a brand you intend to take to market in AU proptech, this is a real liability: trademark risk, SEO dilution, brand confusion, and a damaged-adjacency `.com`.

The good news: you are **internal-first for ~6 months with no external customers yet**, so the switching cost is about as low as it will ever be. Now is the cheap time to do this.

## 3. Replacement names — a range to react to

Two caveats up front, both important:

1. **Domains:** I checked 50 candidate domains via a live registrar. 49 came back "not available for purchase" — including words I invented (e.g. `tenably`, `cadenta`). That means the tool reports parked/premium domains as "unavailable", so a red mark here is **not** proof a competitor owns it. Treat the domain column as directional. The reliable reading: **exact-match single-word `.com`/`.ai`/`.io` are effectively unobtainable in 2026** — expect to coin a word or use a modified domain. The one domain confirmed genuinely buyable was `useveranda.com` ($11.25/yr), which proves the "use-/try-/get- + word" pattern works.
2. **The whole point is distinctiveness.** I deliberately avoided the `lease-/leasi-` root (that root is the collision). Names below lean on stewardship, structure (architecture), and Australian identity — all on-brand for "custody of someone's biggest asset, done carefully."

### Tier A — Australian identity (my favourites for an AU-first proptech)

| Name | Idea | Fit | Collision risk | Domain reality |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Jarrah** | WA hardwood used in flooring/decking/construction — durable, local, "built to last." | Distinctly Australian, warm, sturdy, trust-signalling. Easy to say/spell. | Low in proptech (some cafés/wineries use it; verify). | `jarrah.com`/`.com.au` likely taken; secure `jarrah.app`, `getjarrah.com`, `jarrahhq.com`, or `tryjarrah.com`. |
| **Veranda** | The Queenslander verandah — quintessential AU home. | Residential warmth, hospitality, "front of the house." | "Veranda/Verandah" is a common word; some hospitality/design uses. | `useveranda.com` **confirmed available**. `veranda.com.au` taken. |
| **Holdfast** | Holdfast Bay (Adelaide) + "hold fast" = security, stewardship, grip. | Strong, memorable, security/trust connotation — great for review-first guardrails. | Some marine/agency uses; verify in software. | Exact `.com`/`.io` taken; coin `holdfasthq`-style or `.app`. |
| **Quay** | Circular Quay; a place where things dock and settle. | Short, premium, unmistakably AU. | Quay (eyewear) is large but a different class of goods. | Exact `.com` gone; needs a compound/modifier. |

### Tier B — Structure & stewardship (evocative, professional)

| Name | Idea | Fit | Collision risk | Domain reality |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Tenure** | "Security of tenure" — the core legal concept of a lease. | Premium, meaningful, professional. Arguably the most on-the-nose without using "lease". | Low-moderate; verify (academic "tenure" noise in search). | Exact gone; coin/modify. |
| **Lintel** | The beam that bears the load above a door/window. | Quiet, sophisticated structural metaphor — you bear the load for owners. | Low; small fintech uses exist — verify. | Exact gone; coin/modify. |
| **Rafter** | Roof beam — shelter, structure. | Warm + sturdy; friendly to say. | A US ed-fintech "Rafter" existed but is defunct; verify. | Exact gone; coin/modify. |
| **Steward / Stewardly** | Stewardship of property; you already use **Stewart** as the internal codename. | Directly on-message for careful, audited operations. | "Steward" is generic and widely used — hardest to own. | Exact gone; coined form needed. |

### Tier C — Coined / abstract (easiest to trademark & own outright)

Invented words avoid every collision above and are the cleanest to register as a trademark. They carry no meaning until you give them one (cf. Xero, Ailo, Stripe). Directions worth generating more of, then clearing:

- **Ten-** root (from tenant/tenure/tenancy): *Tenari, Tenara, Tenova, Tenly, Tenda.*
- **Dwell/abode** root: *Dwelio, Abodely, Domara.*
- **Own/keep** root: *Ownora, Keeptenant, Keyrove.*

Most short coined `.com`s are also gone, so plan to either lengthen slightly or coin something more unusual, then lock it the moment clearance passes.

### Recommendation

If you want **meaning + AU identity**, my top pick is **Jarrah** (distinctive, ownable feel, low collision, unmistakably Australian, "durable/trustworthy"), with **Veranda** as the pragmatic runner-up because a clean domain is confirmed available today. If you want **maximum legal cleanliness**, go **coined (Tier C)** and trademark it.

Pick a longlist of 3–4, run the trademark/handle clearance in §4 step 1, then commit to one. Don't fall in love before clearance.

## 4. The rename plan

### 4.0 The single most important structural fact

The codebase carries **two different names**, and conflating them is the main risk:

- **"Leasium"** — the **public brand**. ~**357 references across ~60 files**. Mostly user-facing strings, a few env values, the logo component, and external-service display names + the `leasium.ai` domain. **This is what changes.**
- **"Stewart" / `stewart`** — an **internal codename**. ~**606 references across ~190 files**: the Python package (`stewart/`), DB user/database (`stewart`, `stewart_test`), S3 buckets (`stewart-local`), `pyproject.toml` package name, `alembic.ini`, `docker-compose`, CI, Makefile. **It is invisible to users.**

**Recommendation: rename the brand only. Leave `stewart` alone.** Renaming the Python package + DB + buckets is a large, high-risk refactor with **zero external benefit** (no user ever sees "stewart"). Bundling it into a brand change is how a 1-day rename becomes a 2-week migration with downtime. If you ever want full internal coherence, do it later as a separate, isolated ticket. The plan below assumes brand-only.

This also means the back end barely says "Leasium": the real backend hits are `APP_NAME`, the SendGrid from-name, the "Leasium Platform" org name, `platform-admin@leasium.ai`, comments, and comms/template copy. The bulk of the 357 are the front end and docs.

## 5. Suggested sequencing

Phase 1 (decide/secure) is the only slow part — it gates everything and depends on clearance. Phases 2–4 are a focused 1–2 day code+data effort given the brand-only scope. Phase 5 is mostly console work you drive; do it with old+new running in parallel. Because there are **no external users yet**, you can move quickly and keep the old domain redirecting as a safety net.

**Do not** rename the `stewart` package/DB as part of this. It's a separate, optional, internal-only refactor with no brand benefit.
